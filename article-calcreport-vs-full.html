CalcReport vs. Full Valuation: Which Do You Need?

AXIS Independent Advisory

The most common question we hear: "Do I need a full valuation, or is there a simpler option?" The answer depends on the purpose and intended use of the analysis. Here is a straightforward framework for making that decision.

Calculation of Value (CalcReport)

A CalcReport is a focused analysis using agreed-upon approaches and assumptions. It produces an indication of value — defensible and standards-compliant, but narrower in scope than a full valuation. Key characteristics:

  • Does not require the analyst to consider all possible approaches — only those agreed upon as appropriate
  • Lower cost and faster turnaround (2–3 weeks typical)
  • Produces a professional report with documented methodology and assumptions
  • Appropriate for most planning, transactional, and internal purposes

The word "calculation" refers to scope, not quality. A CalcReport applies the same analytical rigor, the same professional standards, and the same credentialed expertise as a full valuation. The difference is the breadth of analysis required.

Conclusion of Value (Full Valuation)

A full valuation considers all three valuation approaches — income, market, and asset-based. It produces a formal conclusion of value with complete documentation. Key characteristics:

  • Requires evaluating and documenting all relevant approach and method decisions
  • Higher cost and longer timeline (4–6 weeks typical)
  • Produces a detailed narrative report suitable for external scrutiny
  • Required when the analysis will face regulatory, legal, or institutional review

Decision Guide

CalcReport Is Typically Sufficient For

  • Buy-sell agreement compliance
  • Succession and transition planning
  • Internal strategic decisions
  • Preliminary transaction negotiations
  • Periodic ownership valuations
  • Estate and gift tax planning (consult your tax advisor)

Full Valuation Is Typically Required For

  • Litigation and dispute resolution
  • SBA or bank lending requirements
  • Regulatory compliance mandates
  • Contested divorce proceedings
  • IRS examinations or challenges
  • Situations requiring a formal opinion letter

Can I Start with a CalcReport and Upgrade?

Yes. A CalcReport builds a solid analytical foundation. If circumstances later require a full valuation — because a transaction moves to litigation, or a lender requires a formal conclusion — the work product can typically be expanded rather than restarted. This makes the CalcReport a practical and cost-effective starting point when the ultimate need is uncertain.

What About Broker Opinions and Informal Estimates?

A CalcReport is not a broker's opinion of value (BOV), which is a marketing tool used to list businesses for sale. It is not a rough estimate or a rule-of-thumb calculation. A CalcReport is a professional engagement governed by national standards, performed by a credentialed analyst, with documented methodology and defensible conclusions.

The word "calculation" refers to scope — not quality, not rigor, and not credibility.
